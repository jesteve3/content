    3.5.12. Molahiz Gaffirs.

    —A number of posts of “molahiz gaffirs” are vacant in the Ministry of the Interior. The pay, which is fixed at L.E. 3 to L.E. 5, does not deprive the officer of the right to his pension or his salary of “disponibilite.” The duties of the “molahiz gaffirs” are to make five inspections per week, to report to the markaz, to examine the roads, bye-ways, canals, etc., of his district, and to arrest persons caught in the act of committing crime.

    3.5.13. An Unnatural Son.

    —A notable of Minieh province, Sheikh Ali Saleh Namman, has for some time been greatly troubled by the conduct of his son, who added idleness to insolence, failed completely in his school career, and finally robbed his father of £200, which he spent in riotous living. The remonstrances of the justly indignant sheikh appear to have inspired the boy with the idea of killing his father and sisters, and enjoying the family wealth at his ease. He accordingly mixed powdered datura leaf with their food and when asked to eat with them, refused. Within an hour of the meal, the sheikh and his three daughters showed symptoms of datura poisoning. The principal medical officer of the moudirieh was called in, and recognising the symptoms, asked the son why he would not eat with his family. His answers proving unsatisfactory, the boy was searched and the poison found in his pockets. The sheikh and his daughters, who are still dangerously ill, have been sent to the hospital at Minieh. The son has been arrested,

    3.5.14. THE GRAND MUFTI. CONDITION OF PATIENT IMPROVED.

    The condition of Sheikh Mohamed Abdou still gives rise to grave apprehensions. The venerable patient is very weak and has passed the last 36 hours in a semi-comatose condition, which was unchanged to-day at midday. On the other hand, the fact that his condition is no worse than it was on Saturday gives some ground for hope that he may yet rally and recover.

    Numerous messages of sympathy and inquiries have been sent to the family of the Grand Mufti,and Princes, Ministers, Diplomatic Agents, and a multitude of European and native notables have called at his house at Schutz during the last two days. We wish to express our most sincere hope for Sheikh Mohamed Abdou’s recovery. Egypt cannot afford to lose one of her greatest sons.

    Commenting on the news of the very serious illness of the Grand Mufti, Sheikh Ali Youssef’s organ, “Al Moayad,” writes “We give with the deepest regret and most poignant grief the sad news that has reached us of the Grand Mufti’s health. It has proved impossible to convey the patient to Cairo owing to the increasing gravity of his illness. His many friends and relations are making the most anxious enquiries concerning him. We hope heaven will grant him recovery and a long life.”

    As we go to press we hear that the Grand Mufti’s condition shows signs of improvement.

    3.5.15. THE RIFAHI AFFAIR.

    We hear that Mohamed Bey Saleh, President of the Assiout Court of 1st Instance, has submitted a memorial to the Acting Regent, to the Judicial Adviser, and to the President of the Court of Appeal. In the memorial he claims that his judicial dignity has been offended by the language used by Aly Bey Abou el Fatouh, who represented the Procureur-General, and by Morcos Effendi Fehmy, Bedawi Bey Riffahi’s counsel, ooncerning the Assiout Tribunal’s judgment, during the recent trial before the Court of Appeal. The memorial demands that Morcos Effendi Fahmy should be brought before a council of discipline on the ground of the aspersions cast by him on the character of the Assiout Court. Mohamed Bey Saleh is said to have stated that he would far rather resign his position than be exposed to such attacks on his dignity and that of his colleagues, and see the independence of the judges made a mere name.

    3.5.16. MARSA MATROUH.

    The Government has finally postponed the discussion of the details of the new administration of Marsa Matrouh till after the vacation. It is not expected that any decree regulating the administration of Egypt’s most western port will be issued till the end of the current year.

    We hear that the Bedouins of the district between Marsa Matrouh and Solum have completely recovered from the excitement into which they were temporarily thrown during the spring of the current year, by the raids of Abd el Kader el Kuzza, the noted sheikh of the Derna Hinterland, and by Turkish military movements.

    3.7.3. BUBONIC PLAGUE.

    The plague bulletin for the week ending 8th inst. reports 19 cases of this disease, of which 13 occurred at Alexandria, 4 at Tant-el-Ghezireh, 1 at Damanhour, and 1 at Goreis. Eight of the cases at Alexandria have ended fatally and there are still three Europeans and four natives under treatment in this town. Two deaths have also been reported during the week from Toukh. The number of recoveries amounts to four, namely, one European at Alexandria, one native at Zifta, and two natives at Damanhour. There are at present 25 cases under treatment, 4 being Europeans and 21 being natives. The total number of cases notified since the 1st January amounts to 146, as compared with 701 cases during the corresponding period last year.

    3.9.1. WRECKED PASSENGERS AT PORT SAID. SAVED BY THE CRUISER “RION." THEIR SOMALI HOSTS.

    (From our Correspondent).
    Port Said, Sunday.

    Yesterday afternoon, the Messageries S.S. “Polynesien” arrived from Aden, at which port she had taken on board 250 of the survivors the Wreck of the “Chadoc,” which was lost! on ÷ on 26th June. The S.S. “Iraouaddy,” of the same company, has just arrived from the canal, haying on board the remaining 360, all of whom were picked up by the Russian Volunteer cruiser “Rion” (ex “Smolensk”) on the 1st inst, and landed at Aden, at which port the “Rion” was obliged to coal, she having burned all her woodwork and fittings to enable her to reach Aden.

    In an interview which I had with two of the passengers of the “Chadoc,” I learned that the vessel went ashore at 4 a.m. close under Guardafui on the 26th ulto. and that most of the ship’s boats were broken up.

    The Somalis, many of whom spoke French, having served in the “Haussas,” took the whole number, including 80 children, off the vessel, only one being lost in the rescue operations. The passengers in many cases were able to save their personal effects, and the steward’s department to disembark a quantity of provisions. The natives built them rude huts of twigs and gorse and treated them all with considerable kindness, which belies the general idea entertained that the men of that coast are utter savages.

    After four days spent in the village, they were able to attract the attention of the “Rion” by means of a signal staff erected on a sand-hill to the north of the Cape and were all embarked (save one, who was lost two days before) on the Russian vessel, arriving at Aden all well on the morning of the 4th July.

    By the “Polynesien” there also arrived 190 Russian refugees, who were transferred to the S.S. “Tchihatchoff,” which vessel sailed last night direct for Odessa.

    M. Coullant, principal agent of the Suez Canal Company, together with a number of other residents, amongst whom was Mme Schmidt, wife of the sub-agent, left for France in the “Polynesien,’ which is due to arrive at Marseilles at daylight on Thursday.

    3.9.2. A LOCAL SCANDAL.

    Some two months ago, I noted the destruction of the “Bains Minerve” and the refusal of the authorities to allow a syndicate to build a similar place on account of the reputation which it had gained. Since then, land has been let in large quantities for the erection of bath houses on the beach, and now, instead of the gambling and so on being carried on in one large building, there are at least 20 or 30 in which the wildest of orgies take place nightly. Not content with that, the whole line of beach is now filthy in the extreme and a grave menace to health—and the police do not see that which is plain to every resident.
